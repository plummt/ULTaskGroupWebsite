UL Circadian Recomended Practices Task Group

Purpose

As society moves further from using the sun as our major source of lighting, and there for the driver for syncing to a 24 hour day, (where we wake up around sunrise and go to sleep soon after sun set), and more reliant on electric lighting as our source of lighting, where there is major goal is to provide lighting for a task, but little to no consideration for maintaining a 24 hour day. Studies have shown that if the internal circadian rhythm is misaligned from their wake\sleep schedule a myriad of health problems start arising, from increase in obesity rates, heart rate abnormalities, increase in depression, and many others. Additionally, there may be social requirements that can cause a person to purposefully desync their light/dark cycle from their sleep wake cycle, these requirements (such as job requirements, or school, or leisure) can increase these effects. It then becomes necessary for lighting fixtures to have Recommended Practices for which fixtures are capable of assisting people to maintain their sleep/wake cycle and light/dark cycle, i.e. entrainment.

This task group consisting of various members from Underwriters Laboratories, the Lighting Research Center, and key persons in the industry, will work to develop these Recommended Practices, and provide resources for developing fixtures that meet these Recommended Practices. These Recommended Practices will work to broaden the industry understating the science of circadian entrainment, through the lens of the research conducted at the LRC and other laboratories and developing guidelines for different applications.

Feedback

Current concerns:

  1. Additional third-party application research is needed to show the efficacy of the perposed Recommend Practices
  2. Consensus Among Experts, both in the Task Group, and from the Stakeholders
  3. Identify the gaps in the knowledge and provide resources, apendicies, and addendums for midgating these while additional research is conducted.
